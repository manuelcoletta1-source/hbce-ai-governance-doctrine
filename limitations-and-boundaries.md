# Limitations and Boundaries

## Scope

This section defines the explicit limitations and boundaries of the HBCE AI Execution Governance Doctrine.

The doctrine defines a cryptographic execution governance model.
It does not redefine the nature of artificial intelligence itself.

---

## 1. No Semantic Validation

The doctrine does NOT:

- Validate the semantic correctness of AI output
- Guarantee factual accuracy
- Guarantee ethical correctness
- Guarantee absence of bias

It governs execution traceability, not output quality.

---

## 2. No Model Certification

The doctrine does NOT:

- Certify model weights
- Audit internal neural architectures
- Validate training data provenance
- Inspect provider internal processes

It remains external to model internals.

---

## 3. No Regulatory Certification

The doctrine does NOT:

- Grant regulatory compliance
- Replace legal certification
- Replace sector-specific obligations
- Replace contractual responsibility

It may assist regulatory processes but does not substitute them.

---

## 4. No Legal Liability Transfer

The governance layer does NOT:

- Transfer liability from operators
- Replace human responsibility
- Override contractual obligations

Identity binding clarifies responsibility.
It does not eliminate it.

---

## 5. No Immunity from Misuse

Identity-bound execution reduces ambiguity.
It does not prevent intentional misuse.

Malicious operators remain responsible under applicable law.

---

## 6. Scope Limitation to Execution Traceability

The doctrine governs:

- Identity binding
- Canonicalization
- Hash integrity
- Append-only continuity
- Deterministic verification
- Signature attestation (if enforced)

It does NOT govern:

- Organizational governance models
- Business ethics policies
- Societal AI risk debates
- Political frameworks

---

## 7. Operational Dependency Boundary

The doctrine assumes:

- Secure key management
- Controlled identity issuance
- Correct canonicalization procedures

Improper implementation invalidates guarantees.

---

## 8. Governance Is Not Security

While cryptographic primitives are used,
this doctrine is not a full cybersecurity framework.

It defines accountability traceability,
not complete system security.

---

## Boundary Statement

The HBCE doctrine defines a deterministic, cryptographically attestable execution governance layer.

It transforms AI execution into an accountable operational event.

It does not claim to solve all AI risks.

It defines the minimum structural primitives required
for accountability-critical AI systems.
