# Regulatory Alignment Context

## Scope

This section clarifies the intended regulatory posture of the HBCE AI Execution Governance Doctrine.

This doctrine does not claim regulatory certification.
It defines a governance model designed to be compatible with accountability-driven regulatory environments.

---

## 1. Governance Posture

The doctrine is built on the following structural principles:

- EU-first governance orientation
- Audit-first system architecture
- Identity-bound execution
- Deterministic verification
- Fail-closed default posture
- Hash-only public registry compatibility
- Separation between intelligence and governance layer

These principles align with environments requiring explicit accountability and auditability.

---

## 2. Compatibility with Regulatory Expectations

The doctrine is designed to support environments where the following expectations are present:

- Traceable operational responsibility
- Explicit authority scope
- Evidence generation at execution time
- Verifiable audit trails
- Minimization of exposed data
- Separation of control and execution

The model does not assert compliance with specific regulations.
It provides a deterministic attestation layer that may assist compliance processes.

---

## 3. Data Minimization Posture

The governance layer operates on:

- Canonical forms
- Hash digests
- Identity references
- Policy identifiers
- Signatures

Public registry implementations MAY operate in hash-only mode,
minimizing exposure of operational content.

---

## 4. Vendor Neutrality

The doctrine does not depend on:

- A specific AI provider
- A specific hosting model
- A specific jurisdiction

The governance layer remains logically independent of model providers.

---

## 5. Accountability Boundary

This doctrine governs execution traceability.

It does NOT:

- Certify AI output correctness
- Replace sector-specific regulation
- Replace contractual liability
- Guarantee regulatory approval

It defines a deterministic evidence primitive for AI execution.

---

## Regulatory Positioning Statement

The HBCE doctrine defines a cryptographically attestable execution governance model
intended for environments where accountability, continuity, and auditability are mandatory.

It is a technical governance framework,
not a regulatory instrument.
